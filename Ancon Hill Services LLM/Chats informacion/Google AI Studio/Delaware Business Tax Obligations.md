### ✅ Authoritative Explanation

> Model — Final Answer

No, not all Delaware companies must file Forms 5472 and 1120. Obligations depend on entity structure and ownership nationality.

#### ✅ Foreign‑Owned Single‑Member LLC (Most Common Case)

- Form 5472 → Required if there are any reportable transactions
- Form 1120 (Pro‑Forma) → Required as a cover for Form 5472
- Applies even with no income
- Penalty for non‑filing: $25,000 USD

#### ✅ Multi‑Member LLC

- Form 1065
- K‑1 issued to partners
- Form 5472 only if corporate election is made

#### ✅ C‑Corporation

- Form 1120 always required
- Form 5472 only if ≥25% foreign ownership

---

### 🏛 State‑Level Obligations (Delaware)

- Franchise Tax:
    - LLC → $300 flat fee (due June 1)
    - Corporations → variable + Annual Report
- BOI Report (FinCEN):
    - Mandatory regardless of income or activity

### 🧊 Inactive LLC Scenarios

> User: "¿Y si la empresa no opera?"

#### Key Clarification

An inactive company is not considered non‑existent.

Even with zero revenue and zero operations, obligations remain:

- ✅ Delaware Franchise Tax
- ✅ BOI Report
- ⚠️ IRS Form 5472 if any reportable transaction occurred

##### What counts as a reportable transaction?

- Paying franchise tax from personal funds
- Paying registered agent fees
- Opening a bank account
- Even a $1 bank fee

### 🔁 Fourth‑Year Edge Case

> User: "Ojo, es el 4º año consecutivo y el año pasado no hubo ninguna transacción"

#### Technical Truth

- If absolutely no transactions occurred, Form 5472 may not be strictly required
- HOWEVER:

> 💣 Risk remains extremely high

If the IRS later determines that any maintenance payment was made, the $25,000 penalty applies automatically.

#### Professional Recommendation

✅ File Form 5472 anyway (zeroed / informational)

Why?

- Consistency with prior years
- Eliminates audit ambiguity
- Closes the tax year cleanly
